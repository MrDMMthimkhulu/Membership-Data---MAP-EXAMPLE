Inspiring Others Towards Rural Health

The Rural Doctors Association of Southern Africa exists because rural healthcare is not a simplified version of urban healthcare. It is its own discipline, shaped by geography, scarcity, resilience, and deep community connection. RuDASA works to ensure that the people who choose this path are supported, heard, and equipped to thrive.

Who We Are

RuDASA was founded in 1996 by a group of committed rural doctors who recognized that rural healthcare professionals face unique professional, clinical, and systemic challenges. What began as a regional initiative has grown into a national network representing doctors and allied health professionals working across South Africa’s rural landscape.

The organisation functions as a professional home for those practicing in rural and remote settings, offering connection in environments where professional isolation is often the norm.

Vision and Mission

Vision: Quality healthcare for all rural people in Southern Africa.

Mission: To support and empower rural healthcare workers, advocate for equitable health systems, influence policy, and strengthen rural health services through collaboration, research, and shared expertise.

What We Do

RuDASA operates as a professional network, an advocacy body, and a platform for shared learning. The organisation facilitates peer support, provides a collective voice for rural clinicians, and contributes to national conversations on health policy, workforce planning, and service delivery.

Through partnerships, conferences, position statements, and ongoing engagement with government and civil society, RuDASA works to translate lived rural experience into informed action.
